29th July,2020

When It Rains

When it rains, my day is marred My plans, I’ll have to put on hold The rains come pouring through the windows When for once I forget to close them

Oh No... a very big NO! The lights have also gone out How do I heat the water for my bath? Bathing is a must, you see So into the bathroom I must go

Moments later, with chattering teeth and bursts of Brrrr I bury myself in my warmest clothes Then crawl and lay all snug under the covers.

The Legacy

When the wheels stop turning And the bells refuse to ring When the clock stops ticking And the chimneys stop puffing smoke At the end of the day, when all is said and done And the bones lay rotten

“What next?” is the question that comes to mind Abruptly ended good deeds rewarded by the faint, dying sounds of children’s laughter replays itself over and over like a long forgotten screenplay A gentle rebuke given to correct a naughty deed A silent nod to encourage good deeds A warm smile to congratulate a noble act

A grateful sigh to appreciate a kind venture An emotional hug to welcome a lonesome wanderer Thunderous applause to appraise a wonderful show of excellence A royal command and a worthy example to establish bonds of love and teach how to forgive and let go Enough strokes of the cane to straighten a crooked future of mishap A promise to give an assurance of return A trusting hand to lead the way
